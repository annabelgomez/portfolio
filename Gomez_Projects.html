Image Manipulation

Course: Computer Science Principles
Duration: March 2019
Objective: Design a logo for Dublin High School's Build Club and create an algorithm that will allow the Build Club to upload any pictures they may take and have their personalized logo pasted onto the image.
Type of Project: Team
Role: Team Manager

Our solution was to design a logo for Dublin High School’s Build Club. Our algorithm allows the Build Club to upload any pictures they may take and have their personalized logo pasted onto the image. Additionally, our algorithm allows the Build Club’s images to have an optional border. There are many different options for the border. The Build Club can choose the color of the border, as well as choose to have decorative circles on the corners in the border. The decorative circles can also be modified in color and each one can have its own individual color. This project will unify the Build Club in all of their advertising and outreach.

We made our final logo, and all other versions of our other logos using Google Draw and draw.io. When it came to the images/clip art used for the tutoring representation of our logo, we used images we found online. Each image was properly cited. Every other part of the logo is original. This logo was saved as a PNG file with a transparent background and as a PNG file with a white background (something that we were able to add in draw.io). We wanted to make sure that our logo would capture every essence of what the Build Club is. The gear on the left shows and represents a student tutoring another student, the middle gear represents the Special Olympic Robotic Thrower Machine (SpORT) that allows special needs students to play basketball and soccer more interactively, and the gear on the right represents the Cookies & Conversations and the 3D Printing workshops with a 3D printer printing a cookie. We hope that the DHS Build Club will use this logo in the future to unite their three separate sections in their future advertisement.

We were productive enough to make four different versions of our algorithm. Our first version, Version 1,takes our logo with a transparent background, resizes it, and then pasted on a chosen corner of specified image. In our algorithm, the size of each input image is taken into consideration to ensure that when our logo is resized, it is sized in proportion with the original image. Version 2 of our algorithm does the same thing our Version 1. However, the only difference is that instead of pasting a logo with a transparent background, we edited our logo to have a white background. The reason for this is that in some images, such as the one above, the background can be very busy, making it difficult to see the text on the logo. Another situation is that the color of the background can be a very similar color to the logo, making it hard for the logo to stand out. Thus, with a white background, it is much easier to distinguish the logo’s text. In the algorithm, we also made it so that instead of pasting the image of our logo with the white background and pointy, straight edges, we made it so that it would curve the edges of our logo with the white background. This logo image was still saved in PNG format. Our Version 3 of our algorithm takes our logo with a transparent background, resizes it, and then pastes it onto a specified image in a chosen corner. A black border is also added, along with four filled in, blue circles, in each corner of the image, that match the color scheme of the logo. The color of the border, along with the color of the four circles can be altered according to preferences. The colors that match our color scheme are noted and commented on our code. The inspiration of the circles on each corner of the border was from a pinboard. We wanted each image to appear more playful, instead of having a boring, solid-colored border, so we decided to add “pins” to our images. Finally, our Version 4 does the same thing that our Version 3 does. The only difference is that instead of pasting our logo with the transparent background, it pastes our logo with the white background. This version also does the same thing that our Version 2 does in terms of curving the edges of our logo to make it more aesthetically appealing. With the image above, it makes more sense to have the logo on a white background because the color of the sky is very similar to the logo of the logo. Thus, without the logo being on the white border, it would be very difficult to distinguish between the sky and the logo.


Self-Defense Accessories

Course: Computer Integrated Manufacturing
Duration: June 2018
Objective: Design and model an invention or innovation that would solve a problem in the world.
Type of Project: Team
Role: Team Manager

Our solution consists of three different accessories that are hard, sharp and can be placed almost anywhere on the body. Our first accessory is called the Fierce Flower by Annabel. This flower had a 3D printed base that had a sharp spike in the middle. Flower petals are attached along the sides of the spike, to design it as a flower. A strip of magnetic tape is also attached to the bottom and customers are provided with magnets so that the flower can be attached anywhere. Ideally, this design is most effective on the shoe since it can be used to kick an attacker in the shins or in the groin in order to disable the attacker and escape. Often in an attack, the attacker has hold of the victim's arms or has immobilized the upper body, which is the most likely source of defense. When the spike is on the foot, this gives a victim a chance to fight back with their arms disabled. But due to the magnetic strip and clip, the accessory can be attached almost anywhere.

Our second accessory is called the Bold Bow. The 3D printed stud center is sharp enough to injure an attacker. To disguise the stud, the ribbon is tied onto the sides to make it look like a regular bow. There is a magnetic clip attached to the back of the bow. The accessory tool can be placed anywhere for easy access. Women can attach the bow to their bracelet with the magnetic clip backing. If an attacker tries to grab the women's wrist, they would prick their hand against the Bold Bow and remove their hand. Then, women can make further damage by hitting the attacker's eyes. The bow is created to be placed on the individual and not stored in a bag. Placing the Bold Bow on the individual will make it easier for women to quickly access a tool to defend themselves. The Bold Bow is more effective than the knife bracelet because there is no preparation needed to use the tool. The sharp stud is in the open and ready to be used when needed.

Our third accessory is called the Breaker Bow. This is an entirely 3D printed bow with the two sharp protrusions that are the bottom of the bow. This bow has a strip of magnetic tape attached to the back side and customers are provided with magnets so the bow can be attached almost anywhere. Ideally, this design can be used on your shoes or as a substitute for brass knuckles. The reason it was designed for the shoe is that the way it is oriented, it is ideal for kicking your attacker in the shins. The protrusions are pointing outward, aimed directly at an attacker's shins. The breaker bow is also large enough to be held in the fist with the protrusions between the fingers to pack more power, disable your attacker, and get away. The breaker bow gives women the ability to fight back and increases their chances of surviving an attack unscathed.


Inch Cube Project

Course: Computer Integrated Manufacturing
Duration: September 2017
Objective: Create a solid model of an object or small assembly that you want to print on our 3D printer using only one cubic inch of ASB plastic.
Type of Project: Individual

For the Inch Cubed Project we were instructed to model and 3D print an object that had a volume of 1 cubic inch or less. We were allowed to create any object we wanted as long as it was less than or equal to one cubic inch, creative, and was not larger than 6" x 6" x 4". I decided to make a picture holder for the many Instax Polaroid photos I have. I wanted to design something sturdy but also decorative. I wanted to ensure that my picture holder would be able to have thin enough inserts so that the pictures would be able to go in with ease without falling out. At first, when my part came out of the 3D printer, there was a lot of support material attached so I was not sure if the inserts would come out. In the end, the pictures fit perfectly. I was also concerned with the wall thickness since I had to make it very thin in order to meet the criteria but because of the shelling I did, the holder is sturdy. I decided to cut Eiffel Tower and heart-shaped holes on the front surfaces to make the holder decorative and to decrease its overall volume. I was able to catch my mistake with the Eiffel Tower and make it high enough so that the bottom of the holder was not open but with the heart, I forgot to move it upwards.

Next time, I would probably make a smaller shape so that I could use thicker walls and still meet the criteria volume. This is so that my part can be more sturdy and portable without needing to worry if it's going to break. I would also make sure that the bottom surface is solid (no holes) by moving all the extrusions upwards to the middle of the surface.


Solar Hydrogen Vehicle

Course: Computer Integrated Manufacturing
Duration: November 2016
Objective: Design, build, and test a car made of VEX parts that will run with a hydrogen fuel and solar cells to research which one is most effective.
Type of Project: Group
Project Role: Team Manager
Documentation

For this project, my team and I had to build a car out of VEX parts. Then, we experimented with both hydrogen and solar fuel cells to see which one would be more effective in powering a car. We needed to fix series and parallel configurations for the cells to power the car and build a car that could sustain both types of cells. I was the project manager for this project and was in charge of making sure that everyone did equal work and making sure that all tasks were completed. For this project, I learned more on how to build cars and machines out of VEX parts, which could help me in the future since I want to become a mechanical engineer. This will give me more experience on building things with my hands. Another thing I learned was the difference between series and parallel configurations on breadboards which helps me in the future as well because next time, I will be more experienced on how to power machines I build. One of our main challenges was getting the car to hold the solar and hydrogen cells. We needed to make our car 12 inches long and 5 inches wide and needed to build the car in a way that made it easy for us to quickly change between solar and hydrogen configurations. We were able to build the car with a platform to hold the hydrogen cells and finally, we built a higher platform to hold the solar cells so that they were at an angle where the sunlight would hit them correctly. As a team, we learned that everyone's ideas matter and should be taken into consideration. This led me to show my organization and teamwork skills to make sure that my team was on task, everyone contributed equally, and that everyone's ideas were taken into consideration.


GaragGo

Course: Principles of Engineering
Duration: February-March 2016
Objective: Model additional cars to go along with you train engine.
Type of Project: Group
Project Role: Team Member

For this project, my team was tasked to model virtual trains that are appropriate for kids to play with. I was responsible for modeling my own car that resembles different animals of a usual zoo. I was also tasked with making my own Technical Drawings for this part of the project as well as my part of the presentation. I needed to use Inventor Studio where I made videos of my trains to showcase the different views. During the process of this project, I learned how to read dimensions on technical drawings (part outlines) so that I could make my own train engine parts. Another important task that I learned to do was how to use Inventor Studio to showcase my different modeled projects and how to make more advanced models with more texture to them by making unique personal colors for them. A challenge that I overcame during this project was how to read dimensions. I was very confused at first because I didn't understand what all the symbols and lines meant but I kept on practicing and taking my time to fully understand where I was getting my measurements from and I was finally able to figure out what each dimension meant. At the same time, I was having a hard time using more advanced skills on Inventor so while I was learning how to read dimensions, I also learned how to use/ show different techniques on Inventor such as: adding a picture to a molded part so that it looks like a color, learning how to combine technical drawings, and how to be more creative with my models to make more complicated designs by making my modeling more accurate with new constraints. My leadership skills were also shown during this project. Since it was a group project, I decided to be a group manager. I made sure that everyone was given and doing an even amount of work and that everyone was completing the work assigned on time. For example, I was on a trip for one week of this project and was out of town so I made sure that I was productive so that I could finish all the work that would need to be done that week. I made sure my team was still able to carry on with their work and not be limited to only doing a certain amount of work by leaving them my work. Upon my return, I was able to help my team out with their work so that we could finish on time. As a team, we learned how to understand people's situations when it came to work and help each other out even when we weren't assigned that specific task.


The R-Trac Brush

Course: Intro to Engineering Design
Duration: May- June 20167
Objective: Design and model an invention or innovation that would solve a problem in the world.
Type of Project: Group Project
Project Role: Team Manager

For this project, my team and we had to invent or innovate a product that would solve any problem that existed. We were asked to come up with a product and then model it and present it to our class. For this product, I was a project manager and helped make sure that work was divided evenly, everyone stayed on task, and that all work was completed. When we were first assigned the project, we thought it would be easier to come up with a product but soon learned that when you are faced with a problem, you don't always go with the first idea. There are many different aspects that you need to look at. You need to figure out exactly how the product will work and how the product will change if there is a problem. You need to be flexible and open to new ideas. When you are brainstorming, you can't just grab on to the first idea you like. One of the challenges we needed to overcome was to come up with a product that would truly work. We needed to communicate how exactly the product would work because sometimes, you have an idea in your head but it is hard to share out loud the specific way it would work. To overcome this challenge, we took our time brainstorming and thoroughly thinking about the pros and cons of each product. We learned when to let go of one idea and go on to the next. Some of the special skills that I contributed were creativeness when it came to brainstorming products and organization. I learned that there can be a problem anywhere and to that problem there are solutions. This is what allowed me to think of a variety of different ideas. I also showed my organization skills by setting a due date to everything and making sure my team met that deadline. As a team, we worked well together understanding everyone's standards and strengths. We understood and respected everyone's ideas and made sure that everyone contributed equally.
