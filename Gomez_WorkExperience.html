Work Experience

Engineering Intern at MD Resource

In January, 2019, I obtained a paid engineering internship at MD Resource. This company specializes in developing, manufacturing, marketing, and distributing liposuction systems and supplies. I work 3-4 days a week, after school. I report to and am mentored by the General Manager, who is also a Mechanical Engineer. I’m assigned numerous mechanical engineering projects that require me to apply the theoretical concepts and skills learned in my engineering classes. Additionally, I’ve been introduced to various manufacturing techniques such as vacuum forming, pad printing, and silicon mold making. I just completed my first project which involved testing a new coating material used in the canisters distributed by the company. I conducted liquid, vacuum, and appearance tests to ensure the new coating material met the strict FDA regulations. I maintain a professional Engineering Notebook where I get to write down my observations and findings. This notebook is a crucial part of the process because it allows me to record data that might need to be referenced at a later point. Subsequently, I wrote an official report, detailing my findings, and submitted it to my manager for approval. My next project entails manipulating the pad printing process to see if it can be used in the application of the new coating material. In addition to project-specific tasks, I’ve also been involved in many cleanroom operations and have had the opportunity to learn the mechanics behind various types of equipment built and used by MD Resource. I feel very lucky to have been selected for this internship. Recently, I’ve been doing way more than what’s listed on my job description and I love it!
