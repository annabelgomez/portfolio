3D Printing Workshops

As a member of the Dublin High School's Project Lead The Way Engineering Academy, I have taken various engineering courses, all with the focus of hands-on learning. Every course is project and team-based. These experiences have definitely pushed me outside my comfort zone and have taught me the importance of effective communication; especially when it comes to presenting my ideas. Over the years, I have flourished into a more comfortable speaker who actually looks forward to presenting in front of a crowd; especially when the subject is something that I am passionate about.

During this 2018-2019 school year, I am the leader of the Build Club. I lead the continuation of our Special Olympics Robotic Thrower. I also host workshops to teach my high school peers how to use the various machines, we have at our disposals, such as the 3D printers, Laser Cutter, and CNC Mill. I host these workshops to share my passion for designing and creating practical and useful gadgets. My goal is to create a fun and enjoyable environment where I introduce my peers to the field of engineering.

Above are some of the tests and examples I have made for the purpose of these workshops. Included are examples of some of the slides I use in my presentations when I teach my peers about the machines and technology we will be working with. The red ring-shaped objects are bracelets and rings I have made to test the material NinjaFlex. This material is extremely stretchy and strong and can be used for a variety of purposes, such as making shoes. The pink and purple figures are examples of a filament that can change color when thermally activated. This specific filament is purple when cold and pink when warm. The objects in blue are examples of some personalized key chains I taught my peers how to 3D model and print in our first workshop. And of course, with testing, there are always mistakes.
